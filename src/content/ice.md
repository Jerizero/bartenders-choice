When it comes to ice, the rule of thumb is "the more the merrier". When shaking or straining, the more ice you use, the colder the drink will be whilst not being over-diluted. You have more control over the water content when you use more ice, this is because there will be greater insulation, resulting in a slower melting rate of said ice. 

---

A few notes on ice production:

It is not hard to make your own "block" ice. Find some appropriate sized pieces of Tupperware and fill them with pre-boiled or filtered water. After the water has frozen, use a barspoon or ice pick to break up the larger blocks into your desired sizes.

If the ice is coming out cloudy it means your freezer is too cold, raise the temperature slightly so that the water will freeze at a slower rate, therefore allowing the air bubbles to escape.

It is possible to make large amounts of pre-cut ice in a small freezer. Store cut ice in another container whilst re-using previous containers to freeze \n more ice.

If freezing large blocks of ice for punch bowls, \n try adding slices of citrus or berries into the \n pre-frozen ice.

Please be very careful when using an ice pick. Do not pick ice in hand, use a plastic or steel bowl or container, keeping your other hand clear.
