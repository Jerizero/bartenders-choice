### BUILT (IN GLASS)
Add ingredients to the glass the drink will be served in. The most common example of this is with any Old Fashioned variation. Just add ingredients to whiskey glass, add ice, give it a quick stir and add garnish. 

### FLOAT
To add an ingredient to a drink after the drink has been shaken or stirred and strained off. This practice is most commonly done with drinks being served on ice, the ice allowing the "floated" product to remain on top of the drink. 

### RINSE
To coat the inside of the glass with product (most commonly absinthe) and discard any excess. This works far more effectively if the glass you are using is frozen (straight from the freezer). 

### SWIZZLE
The act of swizzling is to combine ingredients in a drink containing crushed ice. Use a swizzle stick or barspoon to churn ice and ingredients and then add more crushed ice to finish. 

### WHIP OR DRY SHAKE
To combine all non-carbonated ingredients in your shaker and shake without ice. This is used predominantly when making drinks containing eggs. You need to shake drinks containing eggs for a longer period of time than drinks without eggs in order to properly emulsify. This can result in over-dilution of your cocktail. Shaking all the ingredients without ice kick-starts this emulsification process without any initial dilution. This method was created by the fabulous Christie Pope, Milk & Honey, NYC c.2004.
