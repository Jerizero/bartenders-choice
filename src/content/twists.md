---
title: Twists
image: twists.png
---

Always make twists to order. I prefer a swiss-style peeler (with the blades running perpendicular to the handle). Use your right thumb as an anchor and very carefully cut a swatch of citrus peel. Don't dig too deep as you do not want too much pith on your twists. The length of your twists will depend on how the drink is being served. A shorter twist is used for drinks in coupes or cocktail glasses and a longer one used for drinks being served on ice. The person drinking the cocktail should be able to remove the twist from the drink without having to put his or her hands into the liquid. Once you have peeled your twist, hold it about three inches above the glass with the outside of the peel facing the cocktail. Gently pull back the sides of the twist, expressing the oils over the surface of the cocktail. Wipe around the rim of the glass and place in drink, twist side facing upwards.
