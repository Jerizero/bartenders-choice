---
title: How to Shake
image: shake.png
---

Always shake hard and with plenty of ice. We are waking the drink up, not rocking it to sleep. Strain the drink off immediately, letting it sit in the shaker with the cracked ice will further dilute unnecessarily. We always add ice last (after all other ingredients have been measured and poured in) this is to ensure that the dilution process hasn't begun until we are ready for it to begin (it is very easy to get distracted when making drinks).

Water content plays a large factor in how long we shake or stir a drink. You have to factor how much water is going to be added to the drink after it is shaken/stirred to determine how long you will actually shake/stir it for. If a drink is to be strained out over fresh ice, remember that further water will be added in the time it sits on that ice, therefore we should shake this drink for less time than we would shake a drink being served without ice. Furthermore, if a drink is being topped with club soda or champagne and being served on ice, we should shake it for an even shorter amount of time than "up" or "rocks" drinks so as not to create too much dilution.

Here are the basic rules when shaking with \n cracked ice:

**Shaken, "Up" Drinks**:
Hard shake for a minimum of 10 seconds

**Rocks Drinks** (no club soda/champagne):
Hard shake for a minimum of 7 seconds.

**Rocks Drinks** (with club soda/Champagne):
Hard shake for a minimum of 5 seconds.
